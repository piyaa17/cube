Anatomy & Elements

Elements of a Cube 🌸

A cube is a three-dimensional solid object bounded by six square faces, facets or sides, with three meeting at each vertex.

1. Faces

A cube has 6 faces in the shape of a square, and all of them are congruent (equal area). Example: ABCD, EFGH, etc.

2. Edges

A cube has 12 edges of equal length. An edge is a line segment where two faces meet. Example: AB, BC, CG, etc.

3. Vertices

A cube has 8 vertices. A vertex is a point where three edges meet. Example: Points A, B, C, D, E, F, G, H.

4. Face Diagonals

A line segment connecting two opposite vertices on a single face. Every cube has 12 face diagonals. Formula: $s\sqrt{2}$.

5. Space Diagonals

A line segment connecting two opposite vertices through the center of the cube. Every cube has 4 space diagonals. Formula: $s\sqrt{3}$.

Mathematics Formula

Formulas & Calculation Examples ✍️

Cube Volume

$V = s \times s \times s$

Example: Side = $5\text{ cm}$.
$V = 5 \times 5 \times 5 = 125\text{ cm}^3$.
Surface Area

$L = 6 \times s^2$

Example: Side = $5\text{ cm}$.
$L = 6 \times (5 \times 5) = 150\text{ cm}^2$.
